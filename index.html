Paul Goldschmidt

America's First Baseman

Paul Goldschmidt hit 36 home runs in 2017. In 2018, he has already hit 15

The Life of "Goldy"

  • September 10, 1987 - Born in Wilmington, Delaware
  • 2008 - Named Southland Conference hitter of year at Texas State University
  • 2009 - Named both Southland Conference hitter of the year and player of the year while at TSU. As a junior, he was a third team All-American player. Selected by the Arizona Diamondbacks in the eighth round of the 2009 MLB draft.
  • 2011 - Named Diamondbacks Minor League Player of the Year, Baseball America first team All-star, as well as many other achievements.
  • August 1, 2011 - Called up to major leagues
  • 2013 - Selected to play on the NL All-star team
  • 2014 - Became starting first baseman for NL All-star team
  • 2015 - Again was starting first baseman on the All-star team. Won a second Gold Glove and Silver Slugger awards. For the second time was voted runner up to NL MVP, just behind Bryce Harper
  • September, 2015 - First child (a son) born
  • 2016 - Again named to All-star team
  • 2017 - Voted to All-star team for the 5th time. Won the Gold Glove and Silver Slugger awards again.
